\opage{
\otitle{2.5 Pressure of monatomic ideal gas}

\otext

First recall the ideal gas law from classical thermodynamics: $PV = nkT$ (or $PV = nRT$ in terms of moles). In the following, we will
see how this result originates from statistical thermodynamics.

\otext

Pressure is given by Eq. (\ref{eq1.26}):

$$P = kT\left(\frac{\partial\ln\left(Z_{tr}\right)}{\partial V}\right)_{T,n}$$

The partial derivative above can be calculated from the translational partition function (Eqs. (\ref{eq2.13}) and (\ref{eq2.14})):

$$\ln\left(Z_{tr}\right) = N\ln\left(z_{tr}\right) - \ln\left(N!\right)$$
$$\ln\left(z_{tr}\right) = \frac{3}{2}\ln\left(2\pi mk/h^2\right) + \frac{3}{2}\ln(T) + \ln(V)$$

So we get simply:

\aeqn{2.22}{\left(\frac{\partial\ln\left(Z_{tr}\right)}{\partial V}\right)_{T,n} = \frac{n}{V}}

and the pressure is then:

\aeqn{2.23}{P = kT \frac{n}{V} = nkT / V\textnormal{ or }PV = nkT}

}
