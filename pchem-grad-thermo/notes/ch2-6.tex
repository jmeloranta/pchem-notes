\opage{
\otitle{2.6 Enthalpy and heat capacity of monatomic ideal gases}

\otext

Based on Eqs. (\ref{eq1.46}), (\ref{eq2.17}), and (\ref{eq2.24}), we can write the enthalpy as:

\aeqn{2.24}{H = U + PV = \frac{3}{2}nkT + \frac{nkT}{V}\times V = \frac{5}{2}nkT\left( = \frac{5}{2}nRT\right)}

Constant volume and pressure heat capacities can be written accordingly as:

\aeqn{2.25}{C_V = \left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial T}\right)_V = \frac{\partial}{\partial T}\left(\frac{3}{2}nkT\right) = \frac{3}{2}nk \left(= \frac{3}{2}nR\right)}

\aeqn{2.26}{C_P = \left(\frac{\partial H}{\partial T}\right)_P = \frac{\partial}{\partial T}\left(\frac{5}{2}nkT\right) = \frac{5}{2}nk \left(= \frac{5}{2}nR\right)}

where the expressions given in parentheses are written in terms of moles.

}
