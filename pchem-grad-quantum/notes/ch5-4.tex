\otitle{5.4 Group multiplication table}

\otext
A table showing the results from multiplying the symmetry operations is called \textit{group multiplication table}. The number of elements in a group is called the \textit{order} of the group. For example, $C_{3v}$ has the order 6. Note that the symmetry operations do not necessarily commute. If all of the symmetry operations in a group commute, the group is called \textit{Abelian}. Given an object, one can construct the group multiplication table ($C_{3v}$ below):

$E$ & $E$ & $C_3^+$ & $C_3^-$ & $\sigma_v$ & $\sigma_v'$ & $\sigma_v''$\\
$C_3^+$ & $C_3^+$ & $C_3^-$ & $E$ & $\sigma_v'$ & $\sigma_v''$ & $\sigma_v$\\
$C_3^-$ & $C_3^-$ & $E$ & $C_3^+$ & $\sigma_v''$ & $\sigma_v$ & $\sigma_v'$\\
$\sigma_v$ & $\sigma_v$ & $\sigma_v''$ & $\sigma_v'$ & $E$  & $C_3^-$ & $C_3^+$\\
